Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 29, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how VaultNote handles information when you use the app. VaultNote is designed as an offline private notes and password vault app.

VaultNote is designed to store your private notes, passwords, PINs, and other sensitive text locally on your device. The app does not require an account, does not provide cloud sync, and does not intentionally collect or share your personal data.

1. Information We Collect

VaultNote does not intentionally collect personal information from users.

The app is designed to work offline and does not require you to create an account, sign in, or provide personal details such as your name, email address, phone number, or location.

2. Information Stored on Your Device

VaultNote may allow you to save information such as:

  • Private notes
  • Password entries
  • PINs
  • Recovery codes
  • Important text or personal reminders
  • Categories or labels created by you

This information is stored locally on your device. VaultNote does not upload this information to our servers.

3. Local Storage and Encryption

VaultNote is intended to protect your saved information using local app storage and security features such as a master password, PIN, or biometric unlock when available.

Biometric authentication, if used, is handled by your device operating system. VaultNote does not receive or store your fingerprint, face data, or other biometric identifiers.

4. Master Password

VaultNote may use a master password to protect access to your saved information.

For privacy and security reasons, we cannot recover your master password if you forget it. Please keep your master password safe.

5. Backup and Restore

VaultNote may provide backup and restore features so that you can export or import your vault data.

Any backup file created by the app is controlled by you. If you choose to store a backup file in another app, cloud storage service, email service, or external device, that service may handle the file according to its own privacy policy.

You are responsible for keeping your backup files secure.

6. Internet Access

VaultNote is designed to work offline. The app does not require internet access for its core features.

If a future version includes optional online features, this Privacy Policy will be updated accordingly.

7. Third-Party Services

VaultNote does not use advertising services, analytics services, cloud sync services, or account services in its core offline design.

If third-party services are added in a future version, this Privacy Policy will be updated to explain what services are used and what data may be processed.

8. Advertising

VaultNote does not display third-party advertisements.

9. Data Sharing

VaultNote does not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties.

Since your vault content is stored locally on your device and is not uploaded to our servers, we do not have access to your saved notes, passwords, PINs, or other vault content.

10. Children’s Privacy

VaultNote is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

If you believe that a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us so that we can take appropriate action.

11. Data Deletion

You can delete your saved information from within the app, if the app provides deletion features. You can also remove locally stored app data by uninstalling the app or clearing the app data from your device settings.

Because VaultNote does not upload your vault content to our servers, we cannot delete or recover data stored only on your device.

12. Security

We take privacy and security seriously and design VaultNote to minimize data collection. However, no app, device, or storage method can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

Please protect your device, use a strong master password, and keep backup files in a safe place.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.

You are encouraged to review this Privacy Policy periodically.
